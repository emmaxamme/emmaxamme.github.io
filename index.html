What is Tor?

The Tor Project is a non-profit organization that develops and maintains free and open-source software for online anonymity and privacy. Its core technology, Tor (The Onion Router), directs internet traffic through a decentralized, volunteer-operated network, encrypting each hop to obscure the user's location and usage from network surveillance and traffic analysis.

This allows users to browse the internet, communicate, and publish information more privately and securely.

What is a Tor relay?

A Tor relay is a volunteer-run server that forms part of the Tor network. These servers receive encrypted traffic from other Tor users, decrypt one layer to determine the next hop, and then forward it along to another relay or the destination.

By passing traffic through multiple relays, the origin and destination of the data become increasingly difficult to trace, providing anonymity for Tor users.
